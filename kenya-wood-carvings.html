The different types of wood carving

Wood carving is a form of art that has been practiced for centuries. Carvers use a variety of tools to create different types of carvings, from simple designs to complex and detailed pieces. The type of wood used can also vary, with some carvers using softwoods like pine, while others prefer harder woods such as oak or mahogany.
There are many different types of wood carving, each with its own unique style and techniques. Some of the most popular types include:

  • Relief carving: This is a type of carving where the design is raised above the surface of the wood. It is often used for signs, plaques, and other decorative items.
  • Chip carving: Chip carving involves removing small pieces of wood from the surface to create a design. It is a very delicate form of carving and requires a lot of patience and skill.
  • Whittling: Whittling is a type of Kenya wood carvings that uses a knife to remove small shavings of wood from the surface. It is often used to create simple shapes and designs.
  • Pyrography: Pyrography is a type of carving that uses heat to burn designs into the surface of the wood. It can be used to create both simple and complex designs.
  • Intarsia: Intarsia is a type of carving that uses small pieces of wood, called inlays, to create a design. It is often used for decorative items such as jewelry boxes and picture frames.
  • Knot work: Knot work is a type of Kenya wood carvings that involves creating intricate designs using knots. It is often used for decoration, as well as functional items such as bottle stoppers and key chains.
