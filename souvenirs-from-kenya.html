What are souvenirs?

A souvenir is a memento or memento of a past event that is given to someone as a reminder of that event. It can be anything from a simple keychain to an intricate carving. Kenya has a wide variety of souvenirs that can be bought to remember your trip. Some popular ones include wood carvings, beaded jewelry, and fabric paintings.

Traditional Souvenirs from Kenya

When visiting Kenya, be sure to pick up some traditional souvenirs to take home with you! Traditional souvenris from Kenya include carved wooden masks, beaded jewelry, and colorful textiles.
For a truly unique souvenir, pick up a carved wooden mask. These masks are hand-carved by Kenyan artisans and depict various animals and tribal faces. Wear your mask as a decoration or use it as a traditional African mask in ceremonies and performances.
Beaded jewelry is another popular Kenyan souvenir. These handcrafted pieces are made with brightly colored beads and often depict animals or other nature-inspired designs. They make for beautiful and unique pieces of jewelry that you can wear or display in your home.
Colorful textiles are also commonly found in Kenya. Pick up a traditional kanga cloth or purchase a hand-woven basket to take home with you. These colorful items make for beautiful home decor and are great gifts for friends and family back home.

How to haggle for souvenirs from Kenya?

Haggling is a way of life in Kenya. If you're looking to buy souvenris from Kenya, you'll need to be prepared to haggle. Here are a few tips on how to haggle like a pro:

  • Know your limits: It's important to know how much you're willing to spend on a souvenir before you start haggling. Once you've set your limit, stick to it.
  • Start high: When you start bargaining, always begin with a price that's higher than what you're actually willing to pay. This gives you room to negotiate downwards.
  • Smile and be friendly: Haggling can be tough, but it's important to remain friendly throughout the process. Smiling and being polite will help put the seller at ease and make them more likely to accept your offer.
  • Be prepared to walk away: If the seller isn't budging on price, don't be afraid to walk away from the deal. This shows that you're not desperate to buy and that you're willing to walk away if the price isn't right.

What not to buy as a souvenirs from Kenya?

When travelling, it's easy to get caught up in the moment and buy things as souvenirs that we later regret. If you're planning a trip to Kenya, here are a few things you should avoid buying as souvenris from Kenya.

  • Shells: They may be pretty, but buying shells contributes to the destruction of coral reefs. If you must have a shell souvenir, make sure it's from a sustainable source.
  • Animal products: This includes anything made from ivory, skin, fur or feathers. The trade of these products is often unregulated and can contribute to animal cruelty.
  • Fake artefacts: Many fake artefacts are sold as genuine, often at inflated prices. Not only is this misleading, but it also takes business away from legitimate Kenyan artists and craftsmen.
  • Cheap knock-offs: beware of cheap knock-offs of designer goods. These are usually lower quality and support sweatshop labor.
  • Mass-produced tat: resist the urge to buy cheap, mass-produced tat that will end up in the bin. Instead, invest in quality Kenyan handicrafts that will last a lifetime.
